The gods weep for another lost warrior...

The Last Watch

Ragnarok's Edge

A fallen Einherjar, phase-locked to Ravenwatch, sent into endless fractures to hold a dying multiverse together.

The Opening Narrative

"You fell at the edge of the world, blade in hand, breath spent. The Valkyries were coming for you... but something broke.

The Great Bridge did not just break; it splintered. It pierced the veil of other lives, other times, and other worlds. Now the realms are bleeding. The ashes of Muspelheim cool in the neon rain of cities not yet born. The dead of Midgard wander deserts they do not recognize.

Ravenwatch stands at the one stable wound in creation. The Bifrost was not merely a road between realms. It was the loom that held fate in tension. Now every descent is a stitch through a tear that wants to widen.

You were not chosen for Valhalla. You were chosen to be the anchor. One warrior, bound to the shards of the bridge, sent to hunt through the endless echoes and sew the multiverse back together... or watch it all burn."

You are the Echo-Warrior: phase-locked between death and remembrance. Every fall pulls you back to Ravenwatch. Every class is another self leaking through the fracture. The farther you descend, the more the world remembers futures it was never meant to touch.

The Watchers

Huginn, watcher of Thought, measures your combat logic, boons, and the shape of your choices. Muninn, watcher of Memory, keeps the lodge, the stash, and the climb each fracture has remembered.

The Three Fractures

Barrow Descent

Desaturated grave halls, reliquaries, and armored memory made manifest. A cold route where relics and forgotten kings refuse to stay buried.

Ember Gate

Muspelheim collides with an industrial age. Forge heat, steam presses, war-black steel, and shardfire weapons born from the broken bridge.

Seer Hollow

Runic archives, omen chambers, and magical saturation straining against the veil. Thought itself echoes too loudly here.

Beyond Midgard

The Norse fractures are only the beginning. Stabilize these wounds first, then prepare for the neon storms, stagecoach wastes, and machine futures already pressing against the horizon of Ravenwatch.
